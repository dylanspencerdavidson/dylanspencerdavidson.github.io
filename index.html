Walking

The walking should be very standard. Walk in a straight line, and keep turns light and continuous without stopping. The music is in a 4/4 beat, so you can walk in the same tempo as the music. Your facial expression should remain neutral—relaxed and not too strong. During the finale, please make sure the spacing between you and the models in front of and behind you stays tight.

Collection

CFCL continues its creative practice guided by a fundamental question: can fashion contribute to shaping a better society? As a medium that connects society and the individual wearer, clothing functions as an artwork of multiples, allowing the intentions of its maker to spread through the act of wearing. With the VOL.12 Collection, CFCL reconceives the concept of "social sculpture" proposed by Joseph Beuys, marking forty years since his passing. By bringing together social activism and art, Beuys sought to create new forms of society and expanded the definition of art itself. Drawing from his practice, which moves across social impact, creative expression and aesthetic awareness, CFCL layers the conceptual notion of sculpture onto Knit-ware.
